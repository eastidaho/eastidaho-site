Dave's Fire & Smoke BBQ: The Best BBQ in Idaho Falls

Slow-smoked brisket, loaded nachos, and the kind of BBQ mac and cheese that makes you rethink every mac you've ever had.

Food Idaho Falls Recommendation

If you've been looking for real, slow-smoked BBQ in Idaho Falls — the kind where the meat falls apart before the fork even touches it — Dave's Fire & Smoke BBQ is the spot.

Dave's is a food truck parked at the 1sr Street Food Court in Idaho Falls. It's easy to miss if you're not looking for it. But the locals who know, know. And once you've had the brisket, you'll understand why people keep coming back.

What We Ordered

The menu is straightforward and everything revolves around slow-smoked meat done right — pulled pork, brisket, sausage, ribs, and smoked chicken, all prepared with a mix of fruit woods and house seasonings that give it a flavor you won't find anywhere else in the valley.

We tried three things and every one of them delivered.

The Piggy Mac is the move. House-made mac and cheese loaded with slow-smoked pulled pork — the kind of comfort food that makes you close your eyes on the first bite. If you only order one thing, make it this.

The brisket nachos came piled high with meat, cheese, and all the fixings. It's technically a shareable plate, but we didn't share it.

The loaded baked potato with brisket was the sleeper hit. They stuff an Idaho baked potato with slow-smoked brisket, cheese, sour cream, and green onions. It's massive, it's hearty, and it's the kind of thing you didn't know you needed until it's sitting in front of you.

Other standouts on the menu include the pulled pork sandwich on a brioche bun, the Brisket Philly, and the Brisket Mac if you want to upgrade from the Piggy Mac. Sides include southern-style baked beans, potato salad, broccoli salad, and a fresh kale slaw that's a surprisingly good counterbalance to all that smoke.

The Vibe

Dave's isn't a sit-down restaurant with tablecloths and a hostess. It's a food truck — and that's what makes it great. The service is friendly, the prices are fair, and the food speaks for itself. The owners are usually there, and they genuinely care about what they're putting out.

One review we came across put it perfectly: someone with Texas roots and BBQ in their blood said Dave's impressed them. That's not an easy thing to do.

If you've been sleeping on Dave's, this is your sign. Go get the Piggy Mac. Thank us later.
