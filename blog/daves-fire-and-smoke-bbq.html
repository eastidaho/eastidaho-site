Dave's Fire & Smoke BBQ: The Best BBQ in Idaho Falls

Slow-smoked brisket, loaded nachos, and the kind of BBQ mac and cheese that makes you rethink every mac you've ever had.

Food Idaho Falls Recommendation

If you've been looking for real, slow-smoked BBQ in Idaho Falls — the kind where the meat falls apart before the fork even touches it — Dave's Fire & Smoke BBQ is the spot.

Tucked near the corner of 1st Street and Ronglyn Ave, close to Ravsten Stadium, Dave's is easy to miss if you're not looking for it. But the locals who know, know. And once you've had the brisket, you'll understand why people keep coming back.

What to Order

The menu is straightforward and everything revolves around slow-smoked meat done right — pulled pork, brisket, sausage, ribs, and smoked chicken, all prepared with a mix of fruit woods and house seasonings that give it a flavor you won't find anywhere else in the valley.

The Piggy Mac is the move if it's your first time. It's their house-made mac and cheese loaded with slow-smoked pulled pork — the kind of comfort food that makes you close your eyes on the first bite. The Brisket Mac is the upgrade if you want to go all in.

The brisket nachos are another fan favorite — piled high with meat, cheese, and all the fixings. It's a shareable plate, but you probably won't want to share it.

For sandwiches, the pulled pork sandwich is the classic. Slow-smoked pork on a brioche bun with pickles and your choice of sauce. Simple, done right. The Brisket Philly is for when you want something a little different — and it delivers.

Sides include house-made mac and cheese, southern-style baked beans, potato salad, broccoli salad, and a fresh kale slaw that's a surprisingly good counterbalance to all that smoke. Don't skip the loaded baked potato either — they stuff it with pulled pork or brisket, cheese, sour cream, and green onions.

The Vibe

Dave's isn't a sit-down restaurant with tablecloths and a hostess. It's a BBQ joint — and that's what makes it great. The service is friendly, the prices are fair, and the food speaks for itself. The owners are usually there, and they genuinely care about what they're putting out.

One review we came across put it perfectly: someone with Texas roots and BBQ in their blood said Dave's impressed them. That's not an easy thing to do.
