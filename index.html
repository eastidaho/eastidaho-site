Issue #000 · March 29, 2026 · Saturday

Welcome to East Idaho.

This is Issue #000 — our very first newsletter. If you're reading this, you're one of the first people to join us, and that means a lot.

Here's the short version of what we're doing: Every Saturday morning, we're sending you a 5-minute email about the people, places, and stories that make this valley what it is. Honest recommendations, weekend plans, local facts you didn't know, and the kind of stories that don't get told anywhere else.

We're not a news site. We're not competing with anyone. We're just trying to be your most connected friend in the valley — the one who always knows what's going on and where to go.

This is for all 300,000+ of us. Idaho Falls, Rexburg, Rigby, Driggs, Ammon, Shelley, Blackfoot, and everyone in between.

One ask: hit reply and tell us where in East Idaho you're from. We read every single response, and it helps us know who we're writing for.

— Nathan Summers, Cofounder

📷 Photo of the Week

The Water Tower We All Miss

The Idaho Falls water tower — gone but never forgotten. It stood tall over the Snake River for decades, and if you grew up here, you know exactly what it meant to see it on the skyline. 📸 Dan Cutler

💡 Did You Know

Idaho Falls has had hydroelectric power since 1900 — those dams downtown on the Snake River are why your electric bill is so low. Idaho Falls Power is one of the few city-owned utilities in the state, and the rates are some of the lowest in the country.
